History of Technology in Europe

This website is my U2 summative project. It is a website that showcases the history of technology in Europe. With the time period ranging from Roman times to World War 2.

World War 1

For World War 1, I will be talking about two topics, weapons and trench warfare.

Weapons

Because of the addition of trench warfare, new weapons had to be made. Some of these weapons include machine guns, mustard/chemical gas, tanks. As well as towers to fire into the trenches.

Trench Warfare

Trench warfare was a new way of fighting, this involved digging trenches at the battle ground to make it harder for you to be hit by your opponent. There was also a area called "no man's land" between the trenches which was very dangerous and hard to cross. This was party because people would soak it in water and make it very muddy.

Changes in Thinking

I have two topics for changes in thinking: the printing press and the electrical generator.

Printing Press

The printing press was invented by Johannes Gutenberg in the 15th century. It was a machine that could create copies of books this change Europe forever because the press let people who weren't rich read books. Because of this books became more popular and are now a staple of life today.

Electrical Generator

The first electrical generator was invented by Michael Faraday in the 1820s. This "Faraday disk" works by using a magnet to create a current in a wire by turning a handle. This changed the world forever because people are still using electricity today.

Setting the Stage

For this topic I will be talking about the industrial revolution and the first steam engine.

Industrial Revolution

The industrial revolution was a time when machines started to take over the work that humans used to do. And forever changed the way industry worked. This affected Europe by making it so certain products were more widely accessible.

First Steam Engine

The first steam engine was invented by Thomas Newcomen in the 1712. This was very important because it turned into things like cars, planes, and tanks. And made factories in the industrial revolution more efficient.

Middle Ages

For the middle ages I will be talking about trebuchets and their impacts on the middle ages. Trebuchets were a type of catapult that used a counterweight to launch a payload instead of tension like traditional catapults. They were invented in the 11th century and were used to launch heavy rocks over half a mile into a castle wall. This invention demonstrated many physics properties still being used today.
